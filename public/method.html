Method

From public signal to paid intelligence

The site reads public headlines through power, capital, routes, media, and historical memory, then routes each issue toward public analysis, a dossier, a briefing, a memo, or a system audit.

Operating process

The five-step desk rhythm

  1. Collect. Pull current African, diaspora, and foreign-affairs signals from public sources such as AllAfrica Business, CFR regional analysis, official institutions, and trusted local publishers.
  2. Filter. Keep only items inside the Black World scope: Sub-Saharan Africa, Black America, the Black Caribbean, Afro-Latino communities, and Afro-Europe.
  3. Tag. Assign levers such as energy security, jobs pipeline, payment rails, Black capital, diaspora capital, supply-chain control, critical minerals, and media power.
  4. Interpret. Ask what the event changes for readers, creators, operators, institutions, and diaspora decision-makers.
  5. Route. Decide whether the issue belongs as a public article, Gumroad dossier, rapid briefing, custom memo, or media/intelligence audit.
Source discipline

News feeds: used to identify live signals and recurring patterns.

CFR-style sources: used as model references for regional discipline, issue framing, and foreign-affairs seriousness.

Public evidence: linked wherever possible so readers can inspect the underlying signal.

Ali's product: interpretation, synthesis, routing, and decision framing.
