Kenya Fuel Shock and the Politics of Worker Mobility

Kenya's fuel-price pressure becomes politically important when it hits the matatu system. Matatus are not just vehicles. They are the circulatory system for workers, traders, students, market women, families, and small businesses that depend on daily movement.

The hidden variable is time

A fare shock changes more than a household budget. It changes who can reach work, how early a vendor must leave, what a family spends on food, and whether a small business can absorb delivery costs.

Why this belongs in Black World analysis

Mobility costs shape the base layer under markets, wages, household survival, diaspora investment, and the local business climate. The topic is useful when it explains a larger system, not when it narrows the brand to one region.
