AfCFTA Source Guide

This guide lists the types of public and institutional sources used to frame the AfCFTA Implementation Watch. It is not a substitute for legal, customs, tax, or investment advice.

Primary source lanes

AfCFTA Secretariat materials, African Union resources, national trade ministry notices, customs and standards agencies, official payment-system information, and multilateral trade or development institution publications.

How sources are used

Sources are used to identify implementation status, trade friction, rules, standards, payment readiness, and open questions. When a public source does not answer a product-level question, the question remains open.

Reader standard

Readers should look for dates, country applicability, product scope, institution named, and whether a statement is official guidance or analysis.
